The Brown Bear

The brown bear (Ursus arctos) is a large bear species that lives in Europe-Asia and North America. Males are larger and stronger than females. Their fur color ranges from cream to dark brown. Bears have large shoulder muscles for digging and teeth suitable for a diverse diet.

Top 3 Characteristics

  1. Large size and sexual dimorphism – Males are larger and more robust than females
  2. Color variation – Fur color ranges from cream to reddish to dark brown.
  3. Strong digging ability – Has powerful shoulder muscles and large paws adapted for digging.

Natural Habitats

  • Forests – Especially dense coniferous and mixed forests.
  • Mountains – Alpine and subalpine regions with rocky terrain
  • Tundra and taiga – Found in northern areas with sparse vegetation.
